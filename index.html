Dagadu Sheth Ganpati

The Dagadusheth Halwai Ganapati temple is a Hindu Temple located in Pune and is dedicated to the Hindu god Ganesh. The temple is visited by over one hundred thousand pilgrims every year. Devotees of the temple include celebrities and chief ministers of Maharashtra who visit during the annual ten-day Ganeshotsav festival.

Iskon Temple

ISKCON was founded by Abhaya Caranaravinda Bhaktivedanta Swami (born Abhay Charan De) in New York City, USA, in the year 1966. He is considered the spiritual guru of the organization. ISKCON was founded to encourage the practice of Bhakti Yoga or Krishna Consciousness.3 Nov 2023

ShaniwarWada

The Shaniwar Wada was normally the seven-story capital building of the Peshwas of the Maratha Empire. It was supposed to be made entirely of stone. It is located near Dagadusheth Ganpati mandir pune.

Swaminarayan Temple

The BAPS Shri Swaminarayan Mandir in Pune is a stunning temple that was inaugurated in February 2017. It is located on the Mumbai-Bengaluru National Highway 4, Narhe - Ambegaon Road, Pune. The temple is known for its intricate hand-carved stone architecture, built according to the principles of ancient Indian Shilpshastra.

Sarasbag

Saras Baug was built in the 20th century around the temple which was built in the mid-18th century in the middle of a lake that once existed in Pune. The idea of building a Ganesh Temple near the Parvati Temple was conceptualised by Nanasaheb Peshwe. The construction began in 1750 and was completed 34 years later, in 1784.

Lal Mahal

The Lal Mahal, also known as the Red Palace, is one of Pune's most famous historical landmarks. It was originally established in 1630 AD by Shahaji Bhosale for his wife Jijabai and son, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Shivaji Maharaj spent his childhood here and stayed until he captured his first fort

Agakhan

Historically, the palace holds great significance. Mahatma Gandhi, his wife Kasturba Gandhi and his secretary Mahadev Desai were interned in the palace from 9 August 1942 to 6 May 1944, following the launch of Quit India Movement. Kasturba Gandhi and Mahadev Desai died during their captivity period in the palace and have their Samadhis located over there.
